Help Protect Our Wetlands

Protecting and restoring wetlands has many benefits – they can clean pollution, give us recreational areas and a place for cultural practices.

How you can protect wetlands

  • Keep rubbish and garden waste out of wetland areas.
  • Control pest plants and animals in and around wetlands.
  • Maintain the natural flow of water.
  • Clean boats and fishing equipment to stop the spread of didymo (rock snot gross) an invasive algal bloom which is very hard to eradicate.
  • Keep dogs away from wetland areas
  • Walk on boardwalks and existing tracks and clean your footwear.

Organisations You Can Join and Donate to:

Support Forest & Bird

Forest & Bird and 10 other organisations are currently developing a national wetland protection plan. Actions include; stopping further destruction of wetlands, mapping wetland areas and requiring landowners to account for drained wetlands.

Support Wetland Trust


The National Wetland Trust is a charitable organisation which aims to increase the appreciation of wetlands and their values by all New Zealanders. They are currently planning a National Wetland Discovery Centre at Lake Rotopiko near Hamilton.

It will be a wildlife sanctuary with wetland gardens, trails, educational and research facilities. The centre will promote wetlands from all over NZ and how they compare to wetlands around the world.

Support Sea Cleaners

The vision of SEA CLEANERS is to preserve the NZ coastline for the benefit of marine life and for the enjoyment of everyone. They remove rubbish from the sea, harbours and oceans. The began operating in 2002 and have collected over 12.7 million litres of rubbish. You can donate or be a volunteer.

Visit Auckland Council's Biodiversity’s Facebook Page

The Auckland Council has volunteer planting days all over the region. You can find notifications about upcoming planting days on Auckland Biodiversity’s Facebook page.
